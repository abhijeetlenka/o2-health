FAQ

Questions we
get asked a lot.

Everything you want to know before booking. If your question isn't here — just WhatsApp us.

Is Semaglutide safe? I've heard mixed things about Ozempic.
+

Semaglutide is FDA-approved and clinically proven for Type 2 diabetes and obesity. The concerns you've heard are mostly about people using it without medical supervision — wrong dosage, no monitoring, buying online. At O2, every patient is assessed by Dr. Thrivikram before prescription. Dosage is set based on your specific health profile. You're monitored every month. That's the difference between safe medical use and risky self-medication.

What's the difference between Ozempic and Indian generic Semaglutide?
+

The active molecule is identical — Semaglutide. Ozempic is the branded version from Novo Nordisk, priced at ₹8,000–12,000/month. Indian generics are manufactured by companies like Dr. Reddy's, Sun Pharma, and Cipla under government-approved licenses — same pharmacological action, same clinical results, a fraction of the cost. This is exactly how generic paracetamol works the same as Crocin.

I have PCOS / Thyroid. Will this program work for me?
+

Yes — and this is actually where most generic programs fail. PCOS and thyroid-related weight gain have different root causes than standard obesity. Dr. Thrivikram specifically evaluates your hormones, insulin resistance and thyroid levels before designing your plan. Semaglutide has shown strong results in PCOS-related insulin resistance. Your dietitian plan is also customised to your hormonal condition — not a generic chart.

How much weight can I expect to lose and in how long?
+

Clinical studies show 8–15% body weight loss over 3–6 months with Semaglutide + diet intervention. For a person at 90kg, that's 7–13kg in 3 months. Results vary based on your starting condition, adherence to the diet plan, and underlying medical factors. We track your progress every month and adjust the plan to keep you on course. We never promise results — we promise a medically designed system that gives you the best chance.

What are the side effects of Semaglutide?
+

The most common side effects are mild nausea, reduced appetite and occasional digestive discomfort — especially in the first 2–4 weeks as your body adjusts. These typically reduce as dosage stabilises. Serious side effects are rare and are more likely with unsupervised use or wrong dosage. This is exactly why Dr. Thrivikram starts you on a low dose and increases gradually under monthly monitoring. You always have WhatsApp access between visits.

There is no catch. The Starter plan at ₹1,999 covers your doctor consultation, basic dietitian plan, and program entry. The medication cost is separate (Indian generics are ₹500–2,000/month depending on dose). Advanced and Comprehensive plans include more frequent visits and deeper monitoring. We're able to keep costs low because we're a Hyderabad-based clinic — not a VC-funded app with a marketing budget bigger than their care team.
